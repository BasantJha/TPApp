Insurance Benefits

In case of death:

1. As per EDLI Scheme 1976

  • Eligibility:
    • Benefit provided in case of death of an employee while in service who was a member of the scheme at the time of death. (Period of Death: 29 April 2021 - 28 April 2024).
    • Such a member must be in continuous service for more than or equal to 12 months in one or more establishments.
  • Benefits: Maximum benefit up to 7 lakhs.

2. As per EPF Scheme 1952

  • Eligibility: Benefit provided in case of death of an employee who was a member of the scheme at the time of death.
  • Benefit: The nominee of the member would receive an accumulated amount of PF plus interest.

3. As per EPS 1995

  • Eligibility: Benefit provided in case of death of an employee who was a member of the scheme at the time of death.
  • Benefits:
    • Widow/ widower Pension:
      • When a member dies while in service, the widow/widower is entitled to a minimum monthly pension of Rs.1,00
      • In case of death of a pensioner, the pension to his/her widow/widower is 50% of the memberâ€™s pension.
    • Children pension
      • Pension amount equal to 25% of widow/widower pension to each child, subject to a maximum of 2 children at a time.
      • Children pension at a time, till 25 years of age.
      • Any disabled child in the family shall get disabled pension for life apart from the two-child pensions.

4. As per ESI Scheme:

  • For dependents benefit:
    • Eligibility:
      • Deceased Insured person in cases where death occurs due to employment injury or occupational hazards.
        • The dependants' benefit is payable to the dependants as per Section 52 of the Act read with provision of 6(A) of Section 2 in cases where an insured person dies as a result of employment injury (EI).
        • The age of dependants, has to be determined either by the production of Documentary evidence as specified in Regulation 80(2) or the Age certified by Medical Officer In charge of Government Hospital or Dispensary.
    • Benefits:
      • The dependent gets paid at the rate of 90% of the wage in the form of monthly payment to the dependents of a deceased Insured person in cases where death occurs due to employment injury or occupational hazards.
      • The minimum rate of DB w.e.f 01/03/2012 is Rs.1200/- monthly, and these rates of the dependent's benefit are increased from time to time.
  • For Funeral expenses:
    • Eligibility: He/she should be an insured person on the date of death.
    • Benefit:
      • An amount of Rs.15,000/- towards the expenditure on the funeral of a deceased insured person.
      • The amount is paid either to the dependent or the eldest surviving member of the family or in his absence to the person who incurs the expenditure on the funeral of the deceased insured person.

In case of layoff:

  • Under ESIC (for Rajiv Gandhi Shramik Kalyan Yojana):
    • Eligibility:
      The IP/IW should be in receipt of unemployment allowance under Rajiv Gandhi Shramik Kalyan Yojana. The entitlement for unemployment allowance is re-iterated as under:-
      • The person should have been IP for a period as specified by the ESIC. He/she should also be IP on the date of rendering jobless.
      • The IP/IW should be jobless due to retrenchment or closure of factory/establishment or permanent invalidity arising out of non-employment injury of not less than 40%.
      • The IP/IW should not be in gainful employment
    • Benefits:
      • An Insured Person who becomes unemployed after being insured for a certain amount of period (as specified by ESIC) due to the closure of a factory/establishment, retrenchment or permanent invalidity not less than 40% arising out of non-employment injury is entitled to:
        • Unemployment Allowance equal to 50% of wage for a maximum period of up to Two Years during the lifetime.
        • Medical care for self and family from ESI Hospitals/Dispensaries during the period IP receives unemployment allowance.
        • Vocational Training provided for upgrading skills - Expenditure on fee/travelling allowance borne by ESIC.
  • Under EPF:
    • Eligibility: Members who are no longer employed for two months or more.
    • Benefits:
      • Avail advance of up to 75% of the total amount available in their PF account.
      • This facility will financially help members during unemployment and enable them to continue their pension membership, as their EPF accounts are not closed.
  • Pension fund:
    • Eligibility: An eligible member of the scheme who has been unemployed for at least 2 months from the date of exit and has worked for less than 10 years.
    • Benefits: Members can withdraw the pension contribution in EPS.

In case of disability:

  • For temporary disablement benefit:
    • Eligibility
      • An employee who suffers employment injury (EI) or Occupational Disease is certified to be temporarily incapable of working. "Employment Injury" has been defined under Section 2(8) of the Act as a personal injury to an employee caused by accident or occupational disease arising out of and in the course of his employment, being in insurable employment, whether the accident occurs or the occupational disease is contracted within or outside the territorial limits of India.
      • The certificates Required for temporary disablement benefit: Accident Report in form 12, Form 8,9,10, 11 and ESIC Med.13.
    • Benefits
      • It is paid at the rate of 90% of the wage payable so long the disability continues. (From day one of entering insurable employment & irrespective of having paid any contribution in case of employment injury.)
      • Duration of temporary disability benefit: There is no prescribed limit for the duration of TDB. This is payable as long as temporary disablement lasts and significant improvement by treatment is possible. If a Temporary Disablement spell lasts for less than 3 days (excluding the day of accident), the insured person will be paid sickness benefit if otherwise eligible. A special point for IMOs/IMPs is that some IPs may resist taking a Final Certificate, especially before 3 days, for fear of loss of TDB.
  • For Permanent Disablement Benefit
    3. Eligibility
    An insured person who suffers permanent residual disablement as a result of employment injury (including Occupational Diseases) and results in loss of earning capacity. The proper authority for assessing loss of earning capacity for injuries is the Medical Board and for Occupational Diseases, Special Medical Board.
    4. Benefits
    • It is paid at the rate of 90% of the wage in the form of monthly payment depending upon the extent of loss of earning capacity as certified by a Medical Board
    • The duration of permanent disablement benefit may be for the period given by the Medical Board if assessment is provisional or for entire life if assessment is final.
    • A member, who is permanently and totally disabled during the employment shall also be entitled to pension as admissible under paragraph 12 subject to a minimum of Rs. 250/- per month notwithstanding the fact that he/she has not rendered the pensionable service entitling him/her to pension under paragraph 12 provided that she/he has made at least one month's contribution to the Pension Fund. The monthly member's pension in such cases shall be payable from the date following the date of permanent total disablement and shall be tenable for the lifetime of the member.
